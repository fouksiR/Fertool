Uterus

Adenomyosis & Serosal Implants

Endometrial tissue invading the uterine muscle wall or growing on the outer surface affects implantation and pregnancy maintenance.

ðŸŽ¯ Impact on Fertility
Adenomyosis creates abnormal uterine contractions and inflammation that interfere with embryo implantation and early pregnancy.
Clinical Finding
40-60% of endometriosis patients also have adenomyosis, compounding implantation challenges
  • Adenomyosis
    Glands embedded in muscle wall causing painful, heavy periods
  • Superficial Serosal Lesions

Fallopian Tubes

Tubal Blockage & Adhesions

Scarring and fluid accumulation in tubes blocks the pathway for egg and sperm, preventing natural conception.

ðŸŽ¯ Impact on Fertility
Blocked or damaged tubes make natural pregnancy impossible. Even partial damage affects embryo transport and increases ectopic pregnancy risk.
  • Hydrosalpinx
    Tube blocked and filled with fluid - toxic to embryos
  • Peritubal Adhesions
    Scar tissue wrapping tubes, preventing egg pickup
Pelvic Cavity
